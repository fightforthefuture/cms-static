justincarter

Texas teenager, Justin Carter, spent over four months in jail for a sarcastic joke he made on Facebook. His bail was set at $500,000, an outrageous amount that his family was unable to raise. Being away from his family, beaten up and placed in isolation forced the teen into a deep depression, with many seriously concerned for his well-being. Recently an anonymous donor put up the money for Justin’s bail and he was released, but the case has just begun and he is now awaiting trial.

The felony charge of making a “terroristic threat” is a troubling one that multiple teenagers across the country have faced recently for their Facebook posts. These posts fall within the parameters protected by the First Amendment and the prosecutions send a chilling message to the public: watch what you say. Cameron D'Ambrosio, a Massachusetts teen, spent over a month in jail for some rap lyrics he posted on Facebook. With the grassroots efforts of people like you, Cam was released and the charges against him were dropped.

Over 125,000 people signed a petition created by Justin’s mother, demanding that Justin be released and stating that these reckless prosecutions are setting a dangerous precedent. The prosecuting attorneys in cases like Justin's must be held accountable so that no other mother will have to go through the ordeal of her child being locked up for something they said one Facebook.
