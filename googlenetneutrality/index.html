We're Google employees and Internet users and we want Google to use its lobbying for good.

Google helped pioneer open Internet principles, but its public silence on net neutrality is doing harm. Let's take a strong stand for real net neutrality and Title II.

Google: "Let's stand with the Open Internet, let's stand with Title II net neutrality."

The only way to stop fast lanes and slow lanes, something Google itself has pushed for, is for the FCC to stop using the same legal authority that has already lost twice in court and instead put Big Cable back in its place as a telecommunications service. As the FCC moves to make a decision in December, we can make sure they know where Google stands --- with the Open Internet and Title II.

Larry Page, Co-Founder of Google, said it best.

As Google has become a powerful company, it should use its power for good. Larry has been one of the main visionaries of that kind of thinking: "I’m really dismayed. Most people think corporations are basically evil. They get a bad rap. And that’s somewhat correct, if companies are doing the same incremental things they did 20 years ago. But that’s not really what we need. Especially in tech, we need revolutionary change, not incremental change.....

You’re working because you want to change the world and make it better; if the company you work for is worthy of your time, why not your money as well? We just don’t think about that. I’d like for us to help out more than we are."
