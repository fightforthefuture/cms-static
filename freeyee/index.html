The world needs to know.

Amos Yee is being held in shackles in Block 7 of Singapore's Institute of Mental Health, a place reserved for people with the most severe mental conditions.

His mother says he is deteriorating rapidly:

"Amos has always been a chirpy, confident and very vocal child. He is also very creative, and would spend an endless amount of time on something which he sets his mind on. But my son is a different person now."

The government is using psychiatry to bypass the legal system and hold Amos against his will, regardless of his guilt or innocence for any crime. They claim he is having his mind retrained for his own well being and the interests of society, but it's obvious that they are trying to break down a young mind that could one day challenge their authoritarian ways.

Singapore is a closed democracy. It has laws against speaking out against the government in groups of 1 or more. It's main media outlet is state-owned. To express your grievances with the state or any policy, you have to step into a free speech circle in the middle of a park. It is often cited by the US Secretary of State for its gross human rights violations. Can you share this so the world knows what kinds of abuse Singapore exerts on their most innocent and helpless citizens?

Watch the video that got Amos arrested.

Amos was arrested on March 29, 2015, after uploading this video, and charged with "deliberate intention of wounding the religious or racial feelings" and "threatening, abusive or insulting communication."

He sacrificed his freedom to share his thoughts on Lee Kuan Yew, the former Prime Minister of Singapore. Whether or not you agree with everything he says, posting this in an authoritarian country was brave, and it's important that his words are not censored. The fact that he was arrested and subjected to forced "mental reconditioning" for a YouTube video tells us there is something very wrong with Singapore.
