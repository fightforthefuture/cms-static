A direct attack on the First Amendment

The Senate Intelligence Committee (those friendly looking folks pictured at left) went into closed-door session last week to secretly attach language to the annual Intelligence Authorization bill that would require websites to report videos, wall posts, tweets, and other content to the FBI if they think they could be related to terrorism.

Since the bill was drafted in secret, very few people have actually seen it. But according to reports, it would apply to all “electronic communication service providers,” and require them to flag suspicious material for the federal government to review and take further action.

But here’s the thing: speech alone is never a crime, and Internet users should never have to watch what we say for fear of being treated like terrorists or criminals.

Bills like this are a direct attack on the basic freedoms guaranteed in the US Constitution. The “war on terror” has already cost us enough. Let’s make sure they don’t use it to take away our freedom to speak our mind and express ourselves openly as well.
