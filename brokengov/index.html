July 4th: Stop Government Surveillance

A government that takes away our privacy is a government that is broken. Recent leaks have implicated the US Government in massive and illegal tracking and monitoring of people around the world; they've taken unconstitutional and unchecked power over our lives. Join us, online and off, to stop their random search and seizure.

Denial, Disorder, Failure

Checks and balances are necessary to a working democracy. Instead, President Obama is stonewalling, Congress has no oversight, and only secret courts that don't need to disclose have a say. Sound like 1984?

Power to fire anyone

Ever sign a petition? Believe we should have the right to? Now, with PRISM, the gov't knows if you're even slightly political -- simply being tracked chills free speech, silences disagreement; 800,000 people can access PRISM, go rogue, take revenge, target our personal lives.

Everything you do

Ever buy a one-way ticket? Gchat with relatives abroad? In their world, this puts you on a list. Every place you go, every thing you buy. That many records on our lives is power. They're making you question everything you do.

It's time to protest.. hard...

No government should be able to arbitrarily invade our homes and the innocent, and have this much info that enforcement is just about selection. Don't stand for it, join the protests everywhere -- on the streets, the web and TV, by signing up here.

Our Values

The 4th Amendment

"The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized."

Now Broken

PRISM: Total Information Awareness

Under a secret interpretation of FISA and their seret court, the NSA has created the largest intelligence gathering program in history. Once called Total Information Awareness, this program collects and reads vast amounts of information on Americans and foreigners without warrants. The contract we have as citizens with this government is the Constitution, and they have broken it.

It's already bad

The age of Obama

When Obama ran for president, he campaigned for civil liberties and against massive surveillance. He ended up being a deciding vote on the Patriot Act.

This just makes it worse

It's "the era of Bush-Obama

Bush started the Patriot Act's secret courts and unlimited powers. Obama has overzealously used the Espionage Act to increase those powers -- he's used it 6 out of 9 times its ever been used.

There's no such thing as privacy anymore

We've gotten used to signing up for sites that tells us our data is gonna be shared with advertisers. Still, tracking and denying you services is another thing. No one thinks it is fair when you can't buy a house based on your facebook profile.

But, it's the government that can brutally imprison.

Advertisers are one thing, the government is another. The government can brutally imprison anyone. Gving them all of our information, letting them track and monitor us in secret is inviting them to abuse the information they have.

Nothing to Hide

There are so many federal crimes, the Congressional Research Service can't even count them. Innocent people get incarcerated for vaguely defined laws.

It's not just terrorism, it's also same-sex marriage.

It may not seem wrong, but there's plenty of obscure laws you break every day.. Now, when the cops know everything you're doing illegally, enforcements is just about selection.

Being tracked can lead to suicide

The reality of being spied on by the feds is real stress, anxiety, and isolation. We know too many innocent people who have killed themselves under this reality.

This is just the tip of the iceberg.

There's been a whole litany of people being spied on unfairly. If we are seeing this now, what lies behind is deeper disorder, weakness, mismanagement -- the underlying structure is broken and dangerous and fragile. It comes from a political system that is so corrupt, so broken that it can't even see or admit to the problem.

This country is founded on whistleblowers

Like our first, Benjamin Franklin. Whistleblowers keep our very large institutions more accountable to the public interest. No one should blindly trust such large institutions that has no checks and balances.

What powerful, scared men do

Obama is going after Edward Snowden and charging him with being a spy. Is this what powerful men do who don't want their secrets exposed?

If you're a journalist, an activist

Journalists and activists do the work many of us do not.
